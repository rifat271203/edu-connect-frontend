Benzene to benzoic acid
AI Tutor
Chemistry conversion aromatic substitution oxidation context used
Benzene can be converted to benzoic acid through a two-step process. First, benzene undergoes Friedel–Crafts alkylation using methyl chloride and AlCl₃ as catalyst to form toluene. Then, toluene is oxidised to benzoic acid using a strong oxidising agent such as potassium permanganate (KMnO₄), which converts the methyl group into a carboxylic acid group under heating, followed by acidification.

Step-by-step breakdown
01
Friedel–Crafts Alkylation
Benzene → Toluene
Benzene reacts with methyl chloride (CH₃Cl) in the presence of aluminium chloride (AlCl₃) as a Lewis acid catalyst. The catalyst generates an electrophilic methyl carbocation (CH₃⁺), which then attacks the benzene ring via electrophilic aromatic substitution to produce toluene.

02
Oxidation of Toluene
Toluene → Benzoic acid
Toluene is oxidised using potassium permanganate (KMnO₄) under acidic or neutral conditions with heating. The methyl group (–CH₃) attached to the benzene ring is fully oxidised to a carboxylic acid group (–COOH), yielding benzoic acid after acidification.
